Sensor Models

Your controller has been reading truth. Real flight computers never get truth — they get an IMU.

DAY08

Objectives

  • Describe what each sensor measures: 3-axis gyro → angular velocity (body frame); 3-axis accelerometer → specific force, not acceleration (it reads +1 g sitting still and ~0 in free fall — reason through why); barometer → pressure → altitude AGL.
  • Model MEMS gyro errors: white noise, constant bias, (optionally) bias random walk. Show how integrating a biased gyro makes attitude error grow linearly with time — the drift problem Day 9 solves.
  • Model barometer noise/quantization and its slower update rate.
Primer Every result so far has cheated in one specific way: the controller has been reading true attitude straight out of the plant. No real flight computer gets that — it gets three noisy, imperfect sensors, and today's job is modeling exactly how they lie. The gyro measures angular velocity directly and is the workhorse for short-term attitude tracking, but every MEMS gyro carries a constant bias plus white noise, and because attitude comes from integrating rate, a constant bias integrates into an attitude error that grows linearly, without bound, forever — the drift problem that motivates tomorrow's estimator. The accelerometer is the one everyone gets wrong at first: it does not measure acceleration, it measures specific force — the non-gravitational force per unit mass — which is why it reads +1 g sitting still on a table (the normal force from the table) and reads ~0 in free fall (no contact force at all). That distinction is why the accelerometer is only a trustworthy attitude reference when the vehicle isn't accelerating much relative to gravity — a caveat that matters enormously once a motor is thrusting. The barometer rounds out the suite with a slower, noisier read on altitude. Parameterizing all three from real datasheets today means the noise your Day 9 filter has to fight is realistic, not a number you made up.

Do

  • Build the Sensors subsystem: truth in → noisy, biased, sampled measurements out. Parameterize noise levels from the BNO085 and BMP390 datasheets (see hardware).
  • Feed raw gyro-integrated attitude to your controller and watch performance degrade; quantify the attitude drift rate for your chosen bias.
  • Notebook entry: which sensor errors matter most for a 10–30 second flight, and which can you ignore?
