Hardware & Parts

Everything needed for the Phase 3 benchtop flight computer, plus the airframe components a follow-on flight project would use. COTS = commercial off-the-shelf. Prices and stock drift — verify before ordering, and substitute equivalents freely as long as the sensor interfaces (I²C/Qwiic) stay the same.

Flight Computer & Sensors

ItemPartRole in the study
Microcontroller + starter kit SparkFun Inventor's Kit (RedBoard)
KIT-15267		 Arduino-compatible dev board, breadboard, jumpers, a hobby servo, and guided circuits for Day 11. Note: v4.1 is retired — order the current revision (v4.1.2, KIT-21301) or any Arduino Uno–class board.
IMU (gyro + accelerometer) SparkFun 6DoF IMU — ISM330DHCX (Qwiic)
SEN-19764		 The navigation heart: 3-axis gyro (angular velocity, body frame) + 3-axis accelerometer (specific force). Days 12–14. High output data rates comfortably support the 100 Hz loop.
Barometric pressure sensor SparkFun Pressure Sensor — BMP581 (Qwiic)
SEN-20170		 Altitude AGL from pressure; very low noise. Used for altitude logging on the bench and essential for any future descent guidance.
Qwiic cables SparkFun Qwiic Cable Kit
KIT-15081		 Solder-free, polarized I²C daisy-chain between the board and both sensors. Eliminates the most common wiring bugs on Day 12.
TVC servos (2×) Blue Bird micro servos (BPS.space's choice) Pitch and yaw gimbal actuation. The kit servo works for Day 14's single-axis bench demo; these are the flight-grade upgrade.
Storage (optional) MicroSD breakout or onboard flash Flight data logging when a USB tether isn't available. Serial-to-laptop logging is sufficient for this study.
Rangefinder (optional) LIDAR distance sensor Precise low-altitude AGL measurement for terminal descent. BPS.space doesn't use one, but it's a promising instructor-suggested addition — a good stretch investigation.
Wiring notes Both sensors share one Qwiic (I²C) chain: board → IMU → barometer. Confirm each device's I²C address with an I²C scanner sketch on Day 12 before debugging anything else. Servos draw more current than a USB port likes under load — power them from a separate 5 V supply or battery pack with common ground to the microcontroller.

Airframe & TVC Mechanism

Benchtop work needs only the TVC mount and servos; the rest of the airframe matters for a follow-on flight project but is worth planning now, since mass properties feed vehicle_params.m.

ComponentApproachNotes
TVC gimbal mountOpen-source 3D-printed designsTwo-axis gimbal holding a 29 mm motor tube; print early — iteration is normal. Measure actual gimbal travel; it sets the saturation limit in the sim.
Nose cone3D printAerodynamics are secondary for a low-speed hop; mass and CG placement are what matter.
FuselageCOTS cardboard tubeStandard model-rocket body tube; cheap, light, easy to cut.
Landing legs3D print / designWide stance, some compliance. A future-project design task.
FinsProbably noneA TVC lander flies too slowly for fins to stabilize it — that's the controller's job. Discuss at MR-1.

Safety

  • No motors in this study. All Phase 3 work is benchtop with electric actuation only. Motor static fires or flights are a separate, instructor-supervised effort under the NAR safety code.
  • Software-limit servo travel before mechanically attaching linkages, so a code bug can't break the mount.
  • Standard lab rules for soldering and battery handling apply.
