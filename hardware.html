Hardware & Parts

Everything needed for the Phase 3 benchtop flight computer, plus the airframe components a follow-on flight project would use. COTS = commercial off-the-shelf. Prices and stock drift — verify before ordering, and substitute equivalents freely as long as the sensor interfaces (I²C / STEMMA QT, Qwiic-compatible) stay the same.

Flight Computer & Sensors

ItemPartRole in the study
Microcontroller Teensy 4.0
PJRC — Teensy 4.0		 600 MHz ARM Cortex-M7 flight computer, programmed through the Arduino IDE via Teensyduino. Pair it with a breadboard, jumper wires, and a hobby servo (any basic prototyping kit) for Day 11's guided circuits.
IMU (gyro + accelerometer) Adafruit 9-DOF Orientation IMU Fusion Breakout — BNO085 (BNO080)
Adafruit 4754		 The navigation heart: 3-axis gyro (angular velocity, body frame) + 3-axis accelerometer (specific force) + magnetometer, with an onboard sensor-fusion coprocessor. Days 12–14. Read the raw gyro/accel for your own complementary/Kalman filter (Days 9 & 13), and optionally compare against its onboard fused quaternion output as a sanity check.
Barometric pressure sensor Adafruit BMP390
Adafruit 4816		 Altitude AGL from pressure; very low noise. Used for altitude logging on the bench and essential for any future altitude-hold guidance.
TVC servos (2×) Blue Bird micro servos (BPS.space's choice) Pitch and yaw gimbal actuation. A basic hobby servo works for Day 14's single-axis bench demo; these are the flight-grade upgrade.
Storage (optional) MicroSD breakout or onboard flash Flight data logging when a USB tether isn't available. Serial-to-laptop logging is sufficient for this study.
Wiring notes Both sensors share one STEMMA QT / Qwiic-compatible I²C chain: Teensy → IMU → barometer. Confirm each device's I²C address with an I²C scanner sketch on Day 12 before debugging anything else. Servos draw more current than a USB port likes under load — power them from a separate 5 V supply or battery pack with common ground to the microcontroller.

Airframe & TVC Mechanism

Benchtop work needs only the TVC mount and servos; the rest of the airframe matters for a follow-on flight project but is worth planning now, since mass properties feed vehicle_params.m.

ComponentApproachNotes
TVC gimbal mountOpen-source 3D-printed designsTwo-axis gimbal holding a 29 mm motor tube; print early — iteration is normal. Measure actual gimbal travel; it sets the saturation limit in the sim.
Nose cone3D printAerodynamics are secondary for a low-speed flight test; mass and CG placement are what matter.
FuselageCOTS cardboard tubeStandard model-rocket body tube; cheap, light, easy to cut.
FinsProbably noneA TVC vehicle flies too slowly for fins to stabilize it — that's the controller's job. Discuss at MR-1.

Safety

  • No motors in this study. All Phase 3 work is benchtop with electric actuation only. Motor static fires or flights are a separate, instructor-supervised effort under the NAR safety code.
  • Software-limit servo travel before mechanically attaching linkages, so a code bug can't break the mount.
  • Standard lab rules for soldering and battery handling apply.
